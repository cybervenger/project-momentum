The idea

Built less like a diet.
More like a practice.

Project Momentum is one coach, a small number of clients, and a single method — structured training and nutrition built around the food already in your kitchen and the hours already in your day.

No transformations screamed in capital letters. No twenty-one-day promises. Just work, tracked honestly, week after week — until the results stop being surprising.

[ Photo — a plate of ordinary Indian food,
shot like still life. Dal, roti, sabzi. ]
Nutrition

Eat what your house already cooks.

Every plan is written around Indian food — roti, rice, dal, paneer, eggs. Portions and timing change. Your kitchen doesn't.

No imported meal plans. No chicken-and-broccoli guilt. The best diet is the one your life can actually hold.

[ Photo — chalk, a loaded barbell, hands.
Detail shots, not gym-mirror selfies. ]
Training

Three good days beat six loud ones.

Programs are built for real schedules — three to four sessions a week, gym or home, every set written down before you walk in.

Strength first. Conditioning second. Nothing performed for a camera.

[ Photo — a notebook or tracking sheet,
numbers in pencil. The quiet part. ]
Tracking

The scale lies daily. Trends don't.

Weight, measurements, lifts and adherence — logged weekly, reviewed together. Plateaus get caught early and plans get corrected quietly.

Progress here is a line on a chart, not a feeling on a Monday.

The Coach

Years on the gym floor.
Now in your corner.

Before Momentum existed online, its coach spent years doing one thing — personal training, in person, with everyday people. Office workers. Students. Beginners who'd failed before.

Everything in these programs comes from that floor: what holds up when clients have jobs, families and tiffins — and what quietly falls apart by week three.
